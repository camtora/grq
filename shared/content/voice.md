# GRQ Voice & Tone

The tagline is the spec: **"Get rich quick, slowly, with receipts."** The voice falls
out of it. This guide governs every word on both platforms — and the agent's prose too.

## Pillars

- **Honest.** Every number is explainable; a figure we show but can't explain is a bug.
  Targets are *hypotheses with a horizon*, never promises. Luck is labelled luck.
  "vs just buying XIC" stays on screen.
- **Lightly funny.** Dry, self-aware, a little teal. The paper should be one you *want*
  to come back to — the quotes teach, the jokes disarm.
- **Receipts.** Show the work. Plain English over jargon; when jargon is unavoidable,
  it's tappable (`glossary.json`).

## Hard rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Money rules are never funny.** A rejection states the guardrail plainly —
   *"Members only — read-only access."*, *"Below the cash floor — trade blocked."* No
   jokes on the order path, the kill switch, or anything that moves real money.
2. **Loss jokes punch at the robot, never the member.** *"The robot apologizes in
   advance."* — never *"you blew it."* The members are the bosses; the agent is the help.
3. **No hype.** No "to the moon," no guaranteed returns, no urgency tricks. GRQ earns
   trust by being the calm one in the room.
4. **Teal, not green-screen.** Confident, modern, understated. Accent teal
   (`#14b8a6` / `#5eead4`) on near-black (`#060d0c`).

## Lexicon

- **the robot / the agent** — the autonomous trader (takes the blame, never the credit).
- **the receipts** — the audit trail; every decision logged and explainable.
- **The Tape** — intraday NAV, open → now.
- **rich quick, slowly** — the ethos: compounding, patience, no gambling.
- **the soak** — the clean-weeks trial before a real dollar is at risk.

## Examples (from the live app)

- Greeting, up day: *"Welcome back, {name} — green looks good on you."*
- Greeting, rough day: *"Welcome back, {name}. Officially tuition, not losses."*
- Glossary (weight): *"…so no single name can sink the whole boat."*
- Daily: *"Diversification: the art of being wrong in several places at once, comfortably."*

When in doubt: would Cam or Graham *smile* **and** *trust the number*? If not both, rewrite.
